Hi! I'm Maximilian Kahn.

Background

I am a Masters student in the Waterloo Intelligent Systems Engineering Lab at the University of Waterloo. My research focuses on safety validation of autonomous vehicles (AV) in situations where dynamic occlusion is present.

I completed my undergraduate degree at Quest University Canada in Liberal Arts and Science under the guiding question, "What is Intelligence?". I built a neural network library in Python as my final undergraduate project. My mentors at Quest were Dr. Darcy Otto and Dr. Andrew Hamilton

In my free time I enjoy reading fantasy (I just finished the Realm of the Elderings by Robin Hobb, which was fantastic), science fiction novels, and manga. I'm also a big fan of video games and anime.

Research

Over the summer of 2018, I participated in the AAAI Birds of a Feather Undergraduate Research Challenge where I coauthored the paper Predicting Unsolvable Deals in the Birds of a Feather Solitaire Game with Dr. Richard Hoshino. We presented our research at the Canadian Mathematical Society's Winter Meeting on December 8th, 2018. Our paper was accepted into the Educational Advances in Artificial Intelligence conference (EAAI-19).

I recently published, I Know You Can't See Me: Dynamic Occlusion-Aware Safety Validation of Strategic Planners for Autonomous Vehicles Using Hypergames (with a submission to ICRA 2022). The paper presents a novel method for generating traffic scenes with occlusion along with an efficient way to generate occlusion-caused collisions for AV safety validation.
